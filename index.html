The Problem

Music education is disappearing from public schools, particularly in underfunded districts. Budget cuts and shifting educational priorities have led to a decline in access to music programs, disproportionately affecting low-income students and communities of color.

The absence of music education deprives students of not just creativity but of developmental tools that help them succeed academically, emotionally, and socially.

The Solution

Nonprofit organizations like Save The Music and Little Kids Rock provide instruments, curriculum, and training to underserved schools. Their programs increase attendance, test scores, and student confidence.
